Vlastislav Hofman

    Vlastislav Hofman was born at Jičín in Bohemia, in February 6, 1884. Hofman studied architecture in Prague from 1902 to 1907.He was a Crech artist and architect, but also known as a painter, set designer, graphic artist, furniture designer, and author. He is best known as an architect strongly influcenced by Cubism. Hofman was also active in avant garde art movements in his homeland, and be associated with artists and writers of the time, including Karel Čapek. Vlastislav wrote many pieces on political subjects and the philosophy of art, exspecially for the journal Právo lidu ("People's Right").His stage designs were mainly for the Vinohrady Theatre (Divadlo na Vinohradich) in Prague. Hofman's design for director Karel Hilar's 1926 production of Hamlet was particularly notable.
